About

Our project is a deep learning endeavor aimed at the detection and recognition of text in real-world scenarios. Initially, we embarked on our journey using the ICDAR2015 and SVT datasets to train the YOLOv8.1 model, striving to fulfill our objectives of robust text detection. However, despite our efforts, the results fell short of our expectations, prompting us to explore more viable alternatives. Through rigorous investigation, we stumbled upon the COCO-Text dataset, which presented a wealth of images and text instances suitable for our purposes. Coincidentally, we also uncovered the release of a new iteration of the YOLO framework: YOLOv8.2. Recognizing this as an opportune moment, we capitalized on it by retraining our model using the COCO-Text database, leveraging a subset of 2000 images. Upon completing the detection phase, we further refined our process by implementing a cropping mechanism to isolate the detected text regions. Subsequently, these cropped text regions were fed into our recognition model, consisting of ResNet and VGG architectures, to ascertain the textual content. Finally, we devised a user-friendly interface to display both the cropped images containing text and their corresponding recognized words, providing a seamless end-to-end solution for text detection and recognition in real-world scenarios." This description outlines the evolution of the project, from initial challenges to the adoption of more suitable datasets and models, culminating in a comprehensive system for text detection and recognition.
